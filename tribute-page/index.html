Tribute to APJ Kalam



About A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, the "Missile Man of India," was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He served as the 11th President of India and was a renowned scientist, known for his work in India's space and missile programs.

Achievements

  • Played a key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Developed the SLV-III, India's first satellite launch vehicle.
  • Received the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997.
  • Authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds."
  • Served as a professor and mentor to young scientists and students.
